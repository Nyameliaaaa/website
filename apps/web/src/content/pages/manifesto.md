## preamble

the internet was once a free and open world where you could be anyone or anything and just simply be and it was fun and you could just explore who people were without being at the whims of a single Brain Soupifying Surface. unless you don't remember that. right you're a normie. you don't. that is on purpose. the Great Internet Merchant Complex has made sure of this. they have destroyed what made the internet and computing great in the name of the frivolous pursuit of "fiduciary duty". the internet was once this magnificent and weird place where people felt free to be themselves, and they actually owned the files on their computer, an era normies have given up too easily. in exchange for what exactly? the normies have not explained what they gained out of this monstorous trade

## section 1: realname policies

before the advent of _facebook_, real names were not commonly present on the internet. myspace for example was a website entirely built on psuedonymity. no one knew you as john smith unless you felt like you wanted your public presentation to BE john smith

_facebook_, being a harvard university project, felt like the concept of psuedonymity on the internet was the greatest mistake to ever exist. for a post grad university world, real name social media makes absolute sense. zucc did not stop at harvard

_facebook_ has convinced millions or even billions of normies that they cannot be on the internet without the exchange of their legal name. this is important not just for the later bits, but just. immediately. here are a few reasons of why this is a disaster:

- it actively harms trans people. a trans person wishing to use _facebook_ whose IRLs are in fact not supportive of trans folx is stuck between:
  a) never having an account that represents THEM on _facebook_ and thus remaining exposed to their deadname while using it,
  b) renaming their current account and outing themselves to people who may physically put them in danger,
  c) attempting to create a new account for their authentic self and either ONE of these accounts gets removed and requires an ID that may not exist to be re-enabled, or
  d) they simply cannot use facebook in the way they choose
- these same options also apply to:
  a) victims of domestic violence
  b) victims of stalking
  c) victims of abuse
- the realname policy also means a drag performer or content creator is left with no recourse for having a presence either. _facebook_ "attempted" to solve this with the ever-confusing-ux of pages and later "professional mode"
- the algorithm used to decide whether a name is "real" is controlled by _facebook_ and cannot be audited. as with any algorithmic decision system that decides on human lives, it is biased. there have been numerous instances of native americans or asian folx denied a _facebook_ account because _facebook_'s ai decided they were not a real person
- sex workers are ALSO affected by this, as it is near impossible to have a sex work identity under SESTA-FOSTA, as if it is reported as a "fake name", ID verification is required, meaning their oppression becomes easier

the damage went beyond preventing large swathes of people unable to use the internet safely, but also majorily destroyed the culture of the internet. if an employer can see everything you've done on the internet, people feel more afraid to be fun or quirky or themselves, and they mutanize themselves into a caricture meant for capitalism's consumption. real name policies are actively destroying your personality. not kidding. think about it. people no longer feel as free to express interests or hobbies or similar to the wider internet in fear of being judged by employers
